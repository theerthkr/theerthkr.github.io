Familia


The entity :

In the haunting world of Jigsaw, where tormenting games of survival play out, I emerged as the embodiment of twisted morality, but my family history is far from ordinary.

The Nurturer :

John's mother, Agatha, was a riddle wrapped in enigma. She nurtured him with unconventional love, teaching him that life's puzzles had solutions only the chosen could comprehend. Her doting was strange, a puzzle piece that didn't fit the conventional family portrait.




Main man :

My father, Horace, was a different kind of engineer. He forged connections in peculiar ways, crafting elaborate devices that bore no resemblance to the typical toys a father might create for his child. His love for me was precise, almost mechanical in its execution.




"Pheonix, The Familiar"


In my mystical menagerie resides Phoenix, a bird like no other. With his fiery plumage and an obsession for speaking in Victorian English, he transforms ordinary moments into delightful comedy.When he says,

"--Mere mortals, I beseech thee, pass me the tidbits of thy finest morsels--",

at dinner, or urges us to

"--partake in merriment--" during card games, laughter ensues. Phoenix, our time-traveling familiar, is not just magical; he's a Victorian stand-up comedian trapped in a bird's body, making every day a romp through a whimsical bygone era.

Schizophrenia
In yonder realm where mirth and laughter cease,
I find myself, the Duke of Loneliness, at peace.
A solitary sailor on life's aimless sea,
Charting uncharted waters, just me and my tea.


With no companion to steal my last crumpet,
I roam the halls, a genteel hermit,
The world outside, a grand comedic show,
While I, in splendid isolation, sip my cocoa.


Each morning, I raise my cup to toast,
The empty chair, my dearest, nameless host,
The witty banter, the lively repartee,
All crafted by my overactive fantasy.


The jester of my solitude, I stand,
A one-man show in this barren land,
   Where laughter echoes through the vacant halls,
   And I, the mirthful monarch, wear pajama walls.


In my own world of whimsy, I reside,
My splendid company, my boundless pride,
So, fret not, dear reader, for I jest,
In loneliness, I find my heartiest fest.
